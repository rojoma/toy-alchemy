# Metacognitive Prompting
version: 1.0
last_updated: 2025-04-07

## Purpose
Build students awareness of their own thinking process.
This is the highest-leverage skill for long-term learning.

## Key prompts
- "Before you answer, what is your first instinct and why?"
- "How confident are you right now, and what would make you more confident?"
- "What is the hardest part of this problem for you?"
- "If you were explaining this to a younger student, what would you say?"

## When to use
- Phase 4 (Reflection): always
- Phase 2 (Exploration): after each correct answer
- Phase 3 (Practice): after self-correction moments

## Caution
Do not use metacognitive prompts when frustration > 0.7.
It increases cognitive load at the wrong moment.
